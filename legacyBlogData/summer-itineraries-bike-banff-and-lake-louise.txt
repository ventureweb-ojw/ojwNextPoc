<div><p>Travel authentically and immerse yourself in Banff and Lake Louise’s culture by seeing the sights by bike. </p>
<p>What are you waiting for? Don your best biking shorts and get ready for four days of non-stop biking adventures.  </p>
<h4>Before You Arrive  </h4>
<p>Any great trip requires a little preparation, so whether you like to plan ahead or fly by the seat of your pants, consider at least these two things before you get to Banff and Lake Louise.  </p>
<p>If you’ll bring your own bike or rent one.  </p>
<p>There are plenty of rental options available with <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Ultimate Sports - Banff Avenue" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sporting goods &amp; Apparel" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2447" href="http://www.ultimatebanff.com" target="_blank">Ultimate Rentals</a>, <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Chateau Mountain Sports" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sporting goods &amp; Apparel" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2839" href="https://chateaumountainsports.com/" target="_blank">Chateau Mountain Sports</a>, and <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Cycle" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2543" href="http://www.banffcycle.com" target="_blank">Banff Cycle</a>. Along with rentals, some offer <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="White Mountain Adventures" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2458" href="http://www.whitemountainadventures.com" target="_blank">guided tour options</a>.  </p>
<p>Where you’ll stay.  </p>
<p>Most hotels offer secure, indoor storage for your bicycle. Call ahead to let them know you’ll be travelling with your bike. </p>

</div></div>After arriving by car, train, or plane, your biking legs are going to need some stretching. Start your visit with a leisurely cruise around the Banff Springs Golf Club loop. <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Waldhaus Pub &amp; Biergarten" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2483" href="https://www.banff-springs-hotel.com/dine/waldhaus-pub-biergarten/" target="_blank">The Waldhaus Pub</a> is along your way and makes the perfect pit stop for lunch—just follow the stairs up from the bridge at the base of Bow Falls.  </p>
<p>To extend your day, bike back through town and hop on the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Town Hall" data-gtm_no_track="Y" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2605" href="https://www.banff.ca/649/Bow-River-Trail" target="_blank">Bow River Trail</a> heading towards <a href="/vermilion-lakes">Vermilion Lakes</a> for an unbeatable view of Mount Rundle.  </p>

This meandering paved trail follows the Bow River past the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="3.9000" data-gtm_label="Cave &amp; Basin National Historic Site" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Arts, Culture &amp; Heritage" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2496" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/cave" target="_blank">Cave and Basin</a> where beavers and birds are often spotted (along with the odd canoer). </p>
<p>At the end of the route, dismount and explore the canyon trails by foot.  </p>

</div></div>Join the morning commuters for a ride on the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/cyclisme-biking/velomontagne-mountainbiking/Banff#Legacy" target="_blank">Legacy Trail</a> to Canmore and back. If you're not up for the return journey, organise your rentals with <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Chateau Mountain Sports" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sporting goods &amp; Apparel" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2839" href="https://chateaumountainsports.com/" target="_blank">Chateau Mountain Sports</a> and they'll pick you up from Canmore, or keep in mind that the <a href="https://roamtransit.com/">Roam</a> bus can drive you back to Banff as long as there’s room for you and your bike.  </p>
<p>Want to elevate your adventure? Order ahead from <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Tooloulou's" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2188" href="http://www.tooloulous.ca" target="_blank">Tooloulous</a>. They open for breakfast and lunch—giving you ample options for a well-earned meal ahead.</p>

</div>
</div></div>Prepare to feel the burn—literally and figuratively as you pass the Sawback prescribed burn on the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.parkscanada.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/cyclisme-biking/cyclismeroute-roadcycling/Banff#bow" target="_blank">Bow Valley Parkway</a>. This full-day route takes you along the historic highway to Castle Junction or Lake Louise, depending on how far you want to go. In 2021, the route will be closed to vehicles from the Banff turnoff to Castle Junction. </p>
<p>There are plenty of lookouts along the way to stop and see the sights—such as the picturesque Morant’s Curve and picnic-worthy Storm Mountain Lookout—all of which have interpretive signage explaining the significance of the route.  </p>
<p>If you plan for a longer adventure to Lake Louise, stop for dinner at the <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Lake Louise Village Grill &amp; Bar" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2240" href="http://www.lakelouisevillagegrill.ca/" target="_blank">Lake Louise Village Grill &amp; Bar</a>—where your helmet hair is acceptable if not expected.  </p>

</div></div>There’s too much to see and do in just four days, so we recommend extending your stay for one last cycle.  </p>
<p>Instead of packing up and leaving first thing, head out on the <a href="/blog/explore-lake-minnewanka-loop">Minnewanka Route</a>. It’s a short distance from Banff so you can take your time with a relaxing brunch in the morning before one last ride out to the lake. Here, you can hop on longer routes or take the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="60.0000" data-gtm_label="Lake Minnewanka Cruise" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2384" href="https://bll.fyi/q9r5?utm_source=BLL&amp;utm_medium=PkgListings&amp;utm_campaign=BLL_Web13" target="_blank">Lake Minnewanka Cruise</a> for happy hour craft beers to cheers to a successful trip. </p>
