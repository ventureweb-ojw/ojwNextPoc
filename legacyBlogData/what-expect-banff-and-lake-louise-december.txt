<div><p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{75}" paraid="494698921">If you’re in search of the quintessential Canadian holiday season, head to Banff and Lake Louise in December.  </p>
<p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{103}" paraid="446691060">Frost hangs on the tips of evergreen trees, elk huddle low in the valley resembling real-life reindeer, and carolers spread cheer up and down Banff Avenue. </p>
<p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{113}" paraid="1437577547">So, come celebrate Bodhi, Hanukkah, Christmas, Kwanzaa, and the New Year with us under dancing night skies streaked in purples and greens of the Northern Lights. </p>
<p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{127}" paraid="354847653">Here’s what you can expect to see, do, and experience in Banff and Lake Louise in December.  </p>

<h4 paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{137}" paraid="1293646757">The Weather  </h4>
<p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{147}" paraid="586855135">Old Man Winter can make an appearance any month of the year, but December is when he sets up camp and calls Banff and Lake Louise home for the season—just in time to usher in winter’s official arrival on December 21.  </p>
<p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{157}" paraid="793886986">Banff Daytime -5°C (23°F)  </p>
<p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{169}" paraid="241851769">Banff Overnight -14°C (6.8°F)  </p>
<p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{177}" paraid="563129861">Lake Louise Daytime -6°C (21.2°F) </p>
<p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{185}" paraid="1181794073">Lake Louise Overnight -19°C (-2.2°F)  </p>
<p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{193}" paraid="1851780680">Expect snow and icy conditions in December. If you’re driving, ensure you have winter tires, use caution, and be prepared to plan around weather conditions. </p>

<h4 paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{203}" paraid="120802579">What to Pack  </h4>
<p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{213}" paraid="943108373">As temperatures consistently rest below freezing, you’ll want to cover yourself from head to toe.  </p>
<p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{225}" paraid="1678382690">If you’re not used to Canadian winters, we suggest packing extra layers (more than you might think reasonable). Wear a toque, neck warmer or scarf, mitts or gloves, a winter jacket, snow pants, and proper winter boots. Underneath, you’ll want long pants, a long-sleeve shirt, and a sweater. </p>
<p paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{235}" paraid="1952331660">If you’re exploring the park, add extra layers like long underwear and bring extra dry clothing with you. It’s essential that you stay warm and dry during the winter, so opt for moisture-wicking fabrics and remove layers as you heat up to avoid sweating through your clothing, which makes you colder. Lastly, make sure that your outer layers are waterproof; precipitation can quickly soak through knit alternatives. </p>

<h4 paraeid="{c1b02d94-f663-4001-a6ea-5318026223c5}{235}" paraid="1952331660">What to See and Do </h4>
<p paraeid="{6daa53e4-583a-4d6d-b841-2790e6568a3a}{8}" paraid="1117987960">You have the best chance of seeing the Northern Lights (Aurora Borealis) during our coldest months from December through February. The best part is that the days are shorter, so there’s no need to stay up all night to catch the show, though they sometimes peak well past dusk. Their visibility is based on weather conditions, so stay more than a couple of nights for better viewing opportunities. Bring your camera and book a private tour with <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Brian Merry Photography" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Professional Services" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2647" href="http://www.brianmerry.ca/" target="_blank">Brian Merry Photography</a>.  </p>
<p paraeid="{6daa53e4-583a-4d6d-b841-2790e6568a3a}{20}" paraid="680245669">During the day, strap on some <a href="/snowshoeing">snowshoes</a> or <a href="/cross-country-skiing">cross-country skis</a> and give winter sports a try now that there’s a good base of snow! There are plenty of beginner, intermediate, and expert <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/etat-sentiers-trail-conditions" target="_blank">trails</a> for you to try on your own with rentals or book a tour with an experienced guide.  </p>
<p paraeid="{6daa53e4-583a-4d6d-b841-2790e6568a3a}{34}" paraid="221376962">The ski hills are also in full swing! Grab a board or a pair of skis and hit up <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="105.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Sunshine Village" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2143" href="https://bll.fyi/1i95" target="_blank">Banff Sunshine</a>, <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="100.0000" data-gtm_label="The Lake Louise Ski Resort" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2603" href="https://bll.fyi/r495" target="_blank">Lake Louise Ski Resort</a>, or <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="65.0000" data-gtm_label="Mt. If that’s not your thing, try <a href="/tubing-tobogganning">tubing</a> or join them for one of their many events.</p>

<p paraeid="{6daa53e4-583a-4d6d-b841-2790e6568a3a}{44}" paraid="927352380">Or rather, find something a little slower tempo and book a visit to the <a href="/spa-and-treatments">spa</a>. Many hotels have in-house spas, or Banff and Lake Louise are home to excellent locally-owned options, too. While you’re looking for a hotel, move “<a href="/blog/relax-outdoor-hot-tubs-banff-and-lake-louise">hot tub with a view</a>” to the top of your list and soak in the mountains without spending more than a second switching between plush robes and hot water.  </p>
<p paraeid="{6daa53e4-583a-4d6d-b841-2790e6568a3a}{54}" paraid="830485478">Finally, in true Hallmark holiday fashion, take a <a href="/sleigh-rides">sleigh ride</a> around Lake Louise drawn by horses. Snuggle up to your significant other and drink hot cocoa as you experience the wonder of Lake Louise in winter. To top it off, stop into <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="The Spirit of Christmas" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Souvenir" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2168" href="http://www.spiritofchristmas.ca" target="_blank">The Spirit of Christmas</a> to find the perfect souvenir for your tree or winter village set. </p>

<h5 paraeid="{6daa53e4-583a-4d6d-b841-2790e6568a3a}{130}" paraid="1202741327">Santa Claus Parade (November 20) </h5>
<p paraeid="{6daa53e4-583a-4d6d-b841-2790e6568a3a}{140}" paraid="1010368372">Children, and children-at-heart, line the sidewalks of Banff Avenue to watch the <a href="/SantaClausParade">Banff Santa Claus Parade</a>. This event is hosted by locals who dress up trailers, company vehicles, and even fire trucks with holiday decor to drive down the main street as they wave, sing, and cheer in their holiday costumes.   </p>
<p paraeid="{6daa53e4-583a-4d6d-b841-2790e6568a3a}{140}" paraid="1010368372">*Due to changing circumstances the Santa Claus Parade is not taking place this year. You can find a full list of planned Christmas events and activities <a href="/Christmas">here</a>.</p>

<h5 paraeid="{6daa53e4-583a-4d6d-b841-2790e6568a3a}{140}" paraid="1010368372">Nightrise</h5>
<p>Starting December 2nd, the skies light up with more than just stars as the Banff Gondola hosts <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="49.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Gondola" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2004" href="https://www.banffjaspercollection.com/attractions/banff-gondola/events/nightrise/" target="_blank">Nightrise</a> in partnership with the Stoney Nakoda Nation. A combination of light, projections, and music cover Sulphur Mountain, telling the story of the First Peoples in this area.</p>
