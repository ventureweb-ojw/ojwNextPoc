<hr/>
<p>If ever there was a time to discover and create new traditions, it’s around Christmas, when the spirit of the holidays inspires you to focus on what’s really important.</p>
<p>Perhaps it’s quality time with loved ones or disconnecting from the hustle and bustle of everyday life. Whatever the holiday season means to you, adopting new Christmas traditions in Banff and Lake Louise is an enriching way to create lasting memories, and ones you can look forward to year after year.  </p>
<p>Here are some of our favourites:   </p>
<h4>1. Take a Time out and Hit the Slopes</h4>
<p>The slopes are often a little quieter on Christmas Day, providing the perfect opportunity to take a few turns, burn some extra calories in anticipation of a big dinner, and keep the little ones active. Spend the afternoon or morning with your nearest and dearest hitting the world-class slopes at one of the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="2099.0000" data-gtm_label="SkiBig3" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2055" href="https://padlock.link/93y9" target="_blank">three mountain resorts</a> in Banff National Park. </p>
<h4>2. Slide Along on a Horse-Drawn Sleigh</h4>
<p>Keep warm under a blanket for a peaceful ride in a <a href="/sleigh-rides">horse-drawn sleigh</a>, an experience the whole family can enjoy. Available in both Banff and Lake Louise, a sleigh ride slows down the pace as you take in the scenery, breathe in the crisp air, and spend time with your loved ones. Plan ahead, check the weather and dress warmly with a hat, scarf, and mitts. </p>
<h4>3. Go for a Fresh Air Picnic</h4>
<p>Traditionally Christmas is a day gathered around the tree indoors, indulging in all the good food life has to offer - but why not adopt a refreshing tradition of time spent outdoors? Take your tradition up a notch and take food with you for an outdoor picnic - don't forget the hot chocolate! Try hiking Tunnel Mountain, snowshoeing around Lake Louise or, for a much longer day, ski touring to Boom or Rockbound Lake. Stock up on supplies in Banff at <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Nesters Market" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2532" href="http://www.nestersmarket.com/banff/" target="_blank">Nesters Market</a> or <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Canada Safeway" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2327" href="http://west.iga.ca/stores/iga-banff/" target="_blank">IGA</a>, or in Lake Louise at the <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="The Village Market" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2174" href="" target="_blank">Village Market.</a></p>

<h4>4. Get Cozy at a cabin</h4>
<p>If you’re into backcountry touring, the <a href="https://www.alpineclubofcanada.ca/web/">Alpine Club of Canada</a> maintains a large network of alpine huts that can be booked throughout the Christmas season. Once you’re at the hut, you can warm up by a wood stove and enjoy an intimate holiday gathering with friends and family, surrounded by nothing but wilderness. Looking for a more catered getaway? Check out one of the <a href="/blog/have-cozy-cabin-holiday-banff-national-park">backcountry lodges</a> in Banff National Park accessible by snowshoe or ski, where warm duvets and gourmet meals await your arrival.</p>
<h4>5. Eat out, you're on Holidays</h4>
<p>One of the best things about Banff and Lake Louise is the mouth-watering food options. They're still available this season, and still offering some of your favourite Christmas dishes, but just a little different. During the holidays, most restaurants are offering delivery, take-away, or curbside pick-up. <h4>6. Skate When the Sun Goes Down</h4>
<p>A few rinks in Banff and Lake Louise are lit at night, making for <a href="/ice-skating">the most magical skating experience</a> imaginable. With Christmas lights twinkling (not to mention stars sparkling above!), you’ll instantly fall in love with this new tradition. When you’re ready to unlace your skates, head in for a well-deserved hot chocolate or a coffee with liqueur. And don’t forget the whipped cream!</p>
<h4>7. Wander the Trails of Banff National Park</h4>
<p>Spend your day trekking through the snow-covered hiking and walking trails of Banff National Park. Whether your mode of transportation is skis, snowshoes or winter boots, there is a trail in Banff and Lake Louise for you. </p>
<p>If you're a local from the Bow Valley, when the sun goes down, immerse yourself in the festive live story <a href="/christmas/christmas-spirit">In Search of Christmas Spirit</a>. Meet Owl, a stunning lantern puppet with a twelve-foot wingspan your guide on this outdoor, night-time experience. </p>
