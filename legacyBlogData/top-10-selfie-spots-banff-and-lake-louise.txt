<div><p>Banff National Park and the Canadian Rockies have dominated Instagram feeds with some of the most beautiful images on the social platform. Iconic stops like Moraine Lake and Lake Louise are uploaded daily while lesser-known spots in Banff National Park surprise those who visit and create equally like-worthy selfies. There are countless locations in Banff and Lake Louise year-round that are the perfect selfie-worthy spots to make your friends jealous – here are our top 10:</p>
<h4>1. Sulphur Mountain</h4>
<p>Mountaintop selfies with panoramic views that are too much to get in one image are found at the summit of Sulphur Mountain. Take an 8-minute ride on the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="49.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Gondola" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2004" href="https://bll.fyi/lhpm" target="_blank">Banff Gondola</a> to the top of this accessible experience and snap your shots on the lookout platform at the upper terminal, or at Sanson Peak – a short hike across a stunning boardwalk. Want to see it for yourself? Check out a 360-degree video <a href="https://youtu.be/1q-E6VJcrlg">here.</a>   </p>

<h4>2. Mt. Norquay Green Spot, sightseeing lift, and the Via Ferrata</h4>
<p>The Cliffhouse Bistro at <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="65.0000" data-gtm_label="Mt. Norquay" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2098" href="https://bll.fyi/9jrv" target="_blank">Mt. Norquay</a> is a true hidden gem of Banff and offers stunning views of Banff and Mount Rundle found nowhere else. Ride the sightseeing chairlift to the top in summer or winter and grab a drink inside the bistro. In the summertime, experience the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="250.0000" data-gtm_label="Mt Norquay's Via Ferrata" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2609" href="https://bll.fyi/2fyw" target="_blank">Via Ferrata,</a> an easy climbing experience that anyone who can hike will enjoy. Climb to the summit of Mt. Norquay while secured to an accessible trail with ladders, steps, handles and suspension bridges. You'll look very hardcore in selfies while all geared up at the top of Mt. Norquay. If a short drive is more on the agenda, stop at the Mt. Norquay Green Spot where two <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/rouge-red" target="_blank">Parks Canada Red Chairs</a> are waiting for the perfect selfie to be taken. </p>

<h4>3. Johnston Canyon</h4>
<p>Waterfall selfies might be the coolest of them all – hike along a suspended walkway through <a href="/johnston-canyon">Johnston Canyon</a> to two sets of waterfalls. Crystal clear water and unique rock formations compliment an already stunning view. And for those that want to capture a unique phenomenon, hike the trail in the winter to enjoy frozen waterfalls. </p>

<h4>4. Rat's Nest Cave at Grotto Mountain</h4>

<h4>5. Sightseeing flight with Alpine Helicopters</h4>
<p>In both summer and winter, <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Alpine Helicopters Inc" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="1965" href="http://www.alpinehelicopter.com/" target="_blank">Alpine Helicopters</a> offer multiple sightseeing flights that take visitors eye-to-eye with glaciers, mountain peaks, lakes, waterfalls, and if you're lucky – some wildlife. Snap a selfie beside the window for a sky-high picture that will definitely make friends jealous. </p>

<h4>6. Vermilion Lakes docks</h4>
<p>Vermilion Lakes are walking distance or a short drive from Banff – there are three lakes that make up the iconic location looking at Mount Rundle, and all three have a dock to hang out on. Third Vermilion Lake has a small hot spring outlet that allows the water to never freeze, always creating the reflections that leave people speechless. </p>

<h4>8. The Icefields Parkway</h4>
<p>Rated among top drives by both Lonely Planet and National Geographic, there are countless locations to stop off at on the Icefields Parkway that are perfect selfie spots. A few of our favourites include Crowfoot Glacier lookout, Bow Lake and the little bridge near the shoreline, Waterfowl Lakes, and the Columbia Icefields. </p>

<h4>10. Lake Louise Ski Resort and Summer Gondola sightseeing lift</h4>
