<div><p>Whether you spend your visit hiking through impressive mountain vistas, contemplating the tumultuous waters of Johnston Canyon, or chasing adrenaline on the ski slope, it’s clear that Banff National Park is a special place.</p>
<p>So special in fact, that it is protected as a UNESCO World Heritage site.</p>
<p>As visitors, it is our responsibility to treat the park with the care it deserves, so future generations will be able to experience the wild beauty of this place.</p>
<h4>Sustainable transportation</h4>
<p>The Banff townsite is very walkable. If you’re visiting for the day, consider parking your vehicle at the<a href="https://banff.ca/parking"> Fenlands parking lot</a> or the <a href="https://banff.ca/1104/Train-Station-Public-Parking">Banff Train Station public parking lot</a> (available May - October) and walking the 10-15 minutes into town. Not only will you benefit from a longer parking time limit, but the walk along the river into town also provides extra opportunities to soak in the glorious mountain views.</p>
<p>Lots of Banff locals use bicycles to get around town and you’ll notice bike racks everywhere once you start looking for them. There’s even a Bike Fix-It station in Central Park if you need to pump up your tires or make a minor repair. Bring your own bicycle or rent one from <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Soul Ski and Bike" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sporting goods &amp; Apparel" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2065" href="https://www.banffsoul.com/">Banff Soul Ski and Bike</a>,<a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Snowtips-Bactrax Ski &amp; Bike Rental" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sporting goods &amp; Apparel" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2063" href="http://www.snowtips-bactrax.com/"> Snowtips-Bactrax</a>, or<a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Ultimate Ski &amp; Ride - Banff Avenue" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sporting goods &amp; Apparel" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2447" href="http://ultimatebanff.com/"> </a><a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Ultimate Ski &amp; Ride - Banff Avenue" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sporting goods &amp; Apparel" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2447" href="http://www.ultimatebanff.com" target="_blank">Ultimate Ski &amp; Ride.</a></p>
<p>In the summer, if you are heading to Banff and Lake Louise from Calgary, take advantage of  <a href="https://www.onitregionaltransit.ca/banff-canmore-service/">On-It</a> busses, and leave your car behind. They provide you with the opportunity to sit back, relax, and enjoy your visit to the mountains, while also reducing the number of cars on the road.</p>
<p>Once you are in Banff, <a href="http://roamtransit.com/">Roam Transit</a> is a great option to travel locally, but also further afield to Lake Louise and Canmore. Buses run regularly to the <a data-gtm_category="Spa &amp; Wellness" data-gtm_event_value="7.3000" data-gtm_label="Banff Upper Hot Springs" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Day Spas" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2330" href="https://www.hotsprings.ca/banff" target="_blank">Banff Upper Hot Springs</a>, <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="49.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Gondola" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2004" href="https://bll.fyi/lhpm" target="_blank">Banff Gondola</a>, and <a data-gtm_category="Accommodation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Tunnel Mountain Village 1 Campground" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Campgrounds" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2569" href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/eng/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/camping/tunnel1.aspx" target="_blank">Tunnel Mountain campgrounds</a>.</p>
<p>In the winter, SkiBig3 offers<a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="2099.0000" data-gtm_label="SkiBig3" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2055" href="https://www.skibig3.com/ski-shuttle/"> free shuttles</a> when you purchase a lift ticket. Shuttles run from Banff and Lake Louise to <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="105.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Sunshine" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2143" href="https://bll.fyi/1i95" target="_blank">Banff Sunshine Village</a>, <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="65.0000" data-gtm_label="Mt. <p>For more great tips on transportation within the park, visit <a href="/explorethepark">Explore The Park</a>. </p>
<h4>Reducing waste</h4>
<p>In addition to wildlife-proof garbage bins, you’ll find <a href="https://banff.ca/ImageRepository/Document?documentID=9891">compost and recycle bins</a> throughout the Town of Banff. Follow the pictures on the side of the bin to learn what goes into each receptacle. And don’t forget to pack out your garbage when you’re hiking.</p>
<p>Make sure to bring your reusable water bottle to test out one of five <a href="http://banff.ca/DocumentCenter/View/4277">water bottle filling stations</a>. The drinking water in Banff is refreshing and delicious.</p>

<h4>Respecting wildlife</h4>
<p>Banff National Park is home to many incredible species, from the tiny Banff Springs snail to the towering moose. </p>
<p>Respect and keep a safe distance away from wildlife to protect both yourself and the animal. Never feed, entice, or disturb a wild animal. <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/banff" target="_blank">Parks Canada</a> have provided <a href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/voyage-travel/conseils-tips/faune-wildlife">ten tips</a> to respect wildlife and keep both you and wildlife safe!</p>
<p>Be bear-aware! Make lots of noise when hiking, carry bear spray and keep your dog on-leash. Parks Canada have lots of practical information for staying safe in bear country on their <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/mtn/ours-bears/securite-safety/ours-humains-bears-people">website</a>.</p>
<p>To learn more about experiencing Banff responsibly, visit the <a href="https://www.banfflakelouise.com/mountaininstitute">Mountain Institute</a>.  </p>

<h4>Eat Locally</h4>
<p>Whilst staying in Banff and Lake Louise, enjoy an authentic food experience by consuming local and regional ingredients. Many of the Banff and Lake Louise restaurants practice in farm-to-table, ensuring the use of local and regional ingredients. For a warm, log cabin dinner surrounded by the national park, <a data-gtm_category="Accommodation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Storm Mountain Lodge &amp; Cabins" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Hotels, Hostels &amp; Inns" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2599" href="http://www.stormmountainlodge.com" target="_blank">Storm Mountain Lodge</a> offer exceptional cuisine that is locally sourced.  For a treat, <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Rocky Mountain Flatbread Company" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Fast Food" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2688" href="http://rockymountainflatbread.ca/" target="_blank">Rocky Mountain Flatbread Co</a> uses local and organic produce, Ocean Wise seafood as well as free-range organic meats. </p>
<p>In the summer, the <a href="http://thebanfffarmersmarket.com">Banff Farmer’s Market</a> is a great source for fresh produce from local and regional farmers. Remember to pack a reusable bag for your delicious finds. </p>
