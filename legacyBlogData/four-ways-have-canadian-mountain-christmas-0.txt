<p>Wish lists and checklists, decorations and celebrations. Amidst the whirl of preparing for the perfect Christmas, the true meaning of the season can be lost. Time with loved ones is precious, and this makes the value of mindful presence ever more important throughout the holiday season. Experiencing a Canadian Mountain Christmas in Banff and Lake Louise is the perfect way to take a step back and take in the moment.</p>
<p>From the heart of Canada’s first national park, you’ll find two vibrant mountain communities that come alive with Canadian Christmas spirit. Every year by mid-November, the town of Banff transforms into a scene from a Christmas snow globe. Decorations adorn the streets, twinkling coloured lights compete with the starlight, and a fresh blanket of snow on the surrounding peaks ensures a truly white Christmas feel. The mountains here are brimming with authentic and festive winter experiences, inviting you to slow down and smell the snowflakes. </p>
<p>Here are four ways to have your very own Canadian Mountain Christmas in Banff National Park. </p>
<h4>1. Feel the Mountains Come Alive with Christmas Activities</h4>
<p>There are plenty of ways to balance calm moments of connection with those of celebration and good ‘ole fashioned Christmas cheer. From mid-November to well after New Year’s Day, Banff National Park offers <a href="/Christmas">many ways to celebrate</a> the spirit of the season. The <a href="/SantaClausParade">Santa Clause Parade of Lights</a> kicks it off, illuminating Banff Avenue with a stream of brightly decorated floats. Peruse local artisan craftwork with a mug of mulled wine in hand at the<a href="/christmas/banff-christmas-market"> Banff Christmas Market</a>, hosted at Warner Stables for two weekends in November. Take a short mountain walk up to <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Centre For Arts And Creativity" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Arts, Culture &amp; Heritage" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2601" href="https://www.banffcentre.ca/event-tags/christmas" target="_blank">Banff Centre</a> to enjoy any number of holiday performances – from A Charlie Brown Christmas featuring The Jerry Granelli Trio to <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Centre For Arts And Creativity" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Arts, Culture &amp; Heritage" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2601" href="http://www.banffcentre.ca" target="_blank">The Debaters Live Christmas Special.</a>   </p>

<h4>2. Enjoy a Mountain Twist on Old Christmas Traditions</h4>
<p>Christmas in the Rockies offers a break from the usual pace of the holidays, but you can still celebrate your favourite traditions in the mountains. Instead of Christmas turkey dinners around the family dining table, roast marshmallows around a crisp winter bonfire as you snuggle up to loved ones. Instead of driving to see the neighbourhood Christmas lights, walk to Banff National Park’s frozen lakes to take in the night sky’s Northern Lights. Instead of decorating the Christmas tree with all the trimmings, take a <a href="/blog/your-guide-winter-hiking-banff-national-park">winter wander</a> through the forest to soak in nature’s nuanced smells and sounds. These mountain moments are sure to linger in your family’s memory for years to come. </p>

<h4>3. Celebrate Christmas Moments in a Mountain Cabin or Castle</h4>
<p>Christmas in the Canadian Rockies celebrates presence over presents, and the atmosphere for creating memorable moments is key. Banff has its very own historical castle built by the CP rail in the early 1900s to do just that, <a data-gtm_category="Accommodation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Fairmont Banff Springs" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Hotels, Hostels &amp; Inns" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2473" href="https://www.christmasatthecastle.ca/?fbclid=IwAR1Ib9Fustj3cJSWDV5KyzFa-4VZTmZu59SAymeyiLIv6EFPqxtttkuW87g" target="_blank">Fairmont Banff Springs</a>. It embodies traditional holiday spirit to the nth degree with <a data-gtm_category="Accommodation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Fairmont Banff Springs" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Hotels, Hostels &amp; Inns" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2473" href="https://www.christmasatthecastle.ca/?fbclid=IwAR1Ib9Fustj3cJSWDV5KyzFa-4VZTmZu59SAymeyiLIv6EFPqxtttkuW87g" target="_blank">Christmas at the Castle,</a> creating a wonderfully festive environment. Daily interactive holiday activities will enchant younger family members, while parents find their own corner of the castle to read or enjoy the snow-covered mountainscapes with a cozy mug of hot cocoa. During your stay, there are several signature dining experiences to enjoy that you’d be hard-pressed to find anywhere else – like Christmas dinner in <a data-gtm_category="Accommodation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Fairmont Banff Springs" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Hotels, Hostels &amp; Inns" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2473" href="https://www.christmasatthecastle.ca/dining?fbclid=IwAR1Ib9Fustj3cJSWDV5KyzFa-4VZTmZu59SAymeyiLIv6EFPqxtttkuW87g" target="_blank">Santa’s suite.</a> For a more secluded feel, spend the night in any of Banff National Park’s <a href="/blog/have-cozy-cabin-holiday-banff-national-park">cozy winter cabins</a> to truly connect with nature. You’ll have no one but the mountains for company. </p>

<h4>4. Take Christmas to the Mountain Top</h4>
<p>Of course, it wouldn’t be a mountain Christmas without the mountains themselves. Place yourself amongst the snowy peaks by visiting any of Banff National Park’s <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="2099.0000" data-gtm_label="SkiBig3" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2055" href="https://padlock.link/93y9" target="_blank">three ski resorts</a>, all just a short journey from the towns of Banff and Lake Louise. All resorts host festive activities, like Christmas feasts, torchlight events, and visiting Santa on the mountain. For a non-ski experience, head to the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="49.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Gondola" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2004" href="https://www.banffjaspercollection.com/attractions/banff-gondola/stories/a-new-family-tradition-christmas-at-the-gondola/" target="_blank">Banff Gondola</a> to take part in their <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="49.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Gondola" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2004" href="https://www.banffjaspercollection.com/attractions/banff-gondola/stories/a-new-family-tradition-christmas-at-the-gondola/" target="_blank">Mountaintop Christmas</a>. At Sulphur’s summit, there’s a world of Christmas to for kids to discover – like cookie decorating, meeting Santa Clause, and short animated Christmas films.  </p>
