<div><p>This summer, adventure awaits those looking to settle in and explore a little longer. Where seekers find a spacious national park brimming with activities to entertain all ages. This three-day itinerary is perfect for families with kids and teenagers and will leave you daydreaming of your next trip to the Canadian Rockies. It can be completed over a weekend or a quieter midweek visit. Although it takes a leisurely pace, this itinerary can be easily adapted to take your adventures to the next level for those seeking an extra thrill.  </p>
<p>Disconnect from the day-to-day and reconnect with nature and your loved ones on this family-favourite escape. </p>

<h4>Day 1: Settle in to Your Surroundings </h4>
<p>After checking into your hotel or campsite, spend the afternoon taking time to soak in the scenery with the kids. If you’re coming through the gates, make sure you collect a <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/serapprocher-connect/xplorateurs-xplorers" target="_blank">Parks Xplorer booklet</a>, it’ll guide the kids' discovery of the park through engaging activities. Plus, they can earn an official collectable souvenir at the end of the trip. </p>
<p>Johnson Lake is a short drive from the town of Banff and offers a family-friendly beach right next to the parking lot. Be sure to pack your swimsuits - the water is warm enough for swimming on hot summer days! Bring along some snacks and inflatable toys (like local stand up paddleboard <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="2099.0000" data-gtm_label="SkiBig3" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2055" href="https://padlock.link/93y9" target="_blank">rentals </a>or simple pool noodles) and soak in the summer sun.  </p>
<p>For the more adventurous families or those with older children, consider renting a canoe from <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="50.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Canoe Club" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2528" href="https://www.banffcanoeclub.com" target="_blank">Banff Canoe Club</a> and paddling up to <a href="/vermilion-lakes">Vermilion Lakes</a>. You’ll get the best seat in the house for stunning views of Mount Rundle.  </p>
<p>Cap off the day with a stroll down Banff Ave and along the Bow River. Don’t forget to fuel up with a treat to beat the heat from <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Beavertails" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Fast Food" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2620" href="http://www.beavertailsinc.com/" target="_blank">BeaverTails</a> or <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Cows Inc." data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2364" href="http://www.cows.ca" target="_blank">Cow’s Ice Creamery</a>.  </p>
<p>Insider Tip: For families with younger children, let them blow off some steam at the local playgrounds. <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Town Hall" data-gtm_no_track="Y" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2605" href="https://banff.ca/833/Central-Park" target="_blank">Central Park</a> offers a conveniently located playground right downtown with fun nature-inspired features.</p>

<h4>Day 2: Enjoy the Great Outdoors </h4>
<p>Today, the real adventure begins! From exploring mountain tops to discovering breathtaking landscapes, the wilderness is at your fingertips with these adventures, easily enjoyed by families with kids.  </p>
<p>Treat the family to jaw-dropping views of the surrounding mountain ranges and Banff townsite. The <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="49.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Gondola" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2004" href="https://bll.fyi/lhpm" target="_blank">Banff Gondola</a> is a great option for those with younger kiddos to get straight to Sulphur Mountain’s summit in no time. Enjoy the engaging discovery centre at the top. If you travel before 12 p.m., one child (6 – 15 years) travels free per paying adult. </p>
<p>Looking for a challenge? <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff#Tunnel" target="_blank">Tunnel Mountain</a> and the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff#Surprise" target="_blank">Hoodoo Trail</a> are great hiking trails for kids, with wide maintained paths. Consider hiking earlier in the morning for a less bustling trail on these popular hikes. Tunnel Mountain, also known by local First Nations as Sleeping Buffalo Mountain, offers several stunning viewpoints, while the less strenuous Hoodoo Trail starts at Surprise Corner and brings you down along the Bow River, ending at interesting geological formations. If you’re after an easier stroll, wander the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff#Fenland" target="_blank">Fenland Trail</a> of <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Town Hall" data-gtm_no_track="Y" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2605" href="https://banff.ca/649/Bow-River-Trail" target="_blank">Bow River Trail</a> close to town. </p>
<p>Insider Tip: Happen to be here on a rainy day? You can find great indoor activities in the town of Banff, like water slides at the<a data-gtm_category="Accommodation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Douglas Fir Resort and Chalets" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Hotels, Hostels &amp; Inns" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2383" href="http://www.douglasfir.com" target="_blank"> Douglas Fir Resort &amp; Chalets</a>, bowling at <a data-gtm_category="Accommodation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Fairmont Banff Springs" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Hotels, Hostels &amp; Inns" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2473" href="https://www.fairmont.com/banff-springs/activities/canadian-bowling-and-entertainment-centre/" target="_blank">Fairmont Banff Springs</a> and <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="High Rollers" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2752" href="http://www.highrollersbanff.com" target="_blank">High Rollers</a>, or several <a href="/historic-sites-museums">museums </a>suitable for kids of all ages. <em>Note, due to current government restrictions, indoor activities are currently closed. </em></p>

<h4>Day 3: Learn About the Local Area </h4>
<p>Wind down your last day in Banff National Park with a more relaxed but equally intriguing activity. Learn about local wildlife by taking a drive (or <a href="/biking">bike</a>) down the<a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/visit/les10-top10/promenade-de-la-vallee-de-la-bow-bow-valley-parkway" target="_blank"> Bow Valley Parkway</a>. The reduced speed limit on this quieter road running adjacent to the Trans-Canada Highway offers more opportunities for wildlife sightings. If you do spot wildlife, help protect them by staying in your vehicle. </p>
<p>Another great activity for older kids and teenagers is a visit to the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="3.9000" data-gtm_label="Cave &amp; Basin National Historic Site" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Arts, Culture &amp; Heritage" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2496" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/cave" target="_blank">Cave and Basin National Historic Site</a>. Daily activities, guided tours, and seasonal special exhibits make learning fun at the birthplace of Canada’s first national park. For a unique perspective, consider taking a nighttime lantern tour.  </p>
