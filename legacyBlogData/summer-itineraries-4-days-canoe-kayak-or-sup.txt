<div><p>Vibrant blue and turquoise lakes are a hallmark image of Banff National Park, so what better way to explore the park this summer than from the water?</p>
<h4>Day 1: Paddle creeks, rivers, and lakes</h4>
<p>You don’t have to go far to find a canoe adventure in the town of Banff. Nestled at the junction of the Bow River and Echo Creek, you’ll find the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="50.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Canoe Club" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2528" href="https://www.banffcanoeclub.com" target="_blank">Banff Canoe Club</a>. Rent a canoe or kayak and paddle upstream on the Bow River. Relax, <a href="/blog/grab-go-restaurants-banff-and-lake-louise">bring some snacks</a> and be rewarded with a gentle float downstream on your return. Or, leaving from the docks, turn right up the meandering Echo Creek to paddle on the stunning <a href="/vermilion-lakes">Vermilion Lakes</a>. Don’t forget your camera – you’ll want to snap photos of Mt. Rundle from your vantage point on the lake. </p>

<h4>Day 2:  Explore the lakes of the Minnewanka Loop</h4>
<p>Stop by the <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="SkiBig3 Adventure Hub" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sporting goods &amp; Apparel" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2056" href="https://bll.fyi/fgq3" target="_blank">SkiBig3 Adventure Hub</a> or <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Radventures Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2979" href="https://www.radventurescanada.com/" target="_blank">Radventures</a> in downtown Banff to rent a stand up paddleboard (SUP). It’s the perfect way to explore Two Jack Lake, just a short drive from town. Take a tip from the locals - leave your car and hop on <a href="https://roamtransit.com/services/lake-minnewanka-service/">Roam Transit’s Route 6 bus</a>. The neighbouring Johnson Lake doubles as a popular swimming spot, so pop in for a dip to cool off on a hot day, and stay a little longer for another outing on the SUP. </p>
<p><a href="/lake-minnewanka">Lake Minnewanka</a> can be a long adventure in itself. Explore by kayak, canoe, or motorboat – all available to <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Lake Minnewanka Boat Rentals" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2971" href="https://www.banffjaspercollection.com/canadian-rockies/banff/lake-minnewanka-boat-rentals/" target="_blank">rent on-site</a>. Visiting Minnewanka by boat offers great fishing – whether you’re experienced or new, pick up a <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/peche-fishing" target="_blank">license </a>and give it a try. </p>
<h4>Day 3:  Canoe the pinnacle of lakes, Lake Louise</h4>
<p>The beauty of <a href="/lake-louise">Lake Louise</a> is irresistible by boat, and starting June 1, you can grab a spot on the <a href="https://roamtransit.com/">Roam Transit </a>Route 8X bus to get a ride straight there. Catch the first bus and enjoy the stillness of the lake in the early morning. Rentals are available on-site at the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="The Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise Boathouse" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2972" href="https://www.fairmont.com/lake-louise/activities/canoeing-on-lake-louise/" target="_blank">Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise</a>. </p>

<h4>Day 4:  Canoe, Kayak, or SUP where the Bow River begins</h4>
<p>If you’ve fallen head over heels for water sports, stop by <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Atmosphere" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sporting goods &amp; Apparel" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="1974" href="https://www.atmosphere.ca" target="_blank">Atmosphere </a>in Banff to outfit yourself for future lake adventures. Already own a canoe, kayak, or SUP, or want to rent one from <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="55.0000" data-gtm_label="Bow Valley SUP&amp;Surf Inc." data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2977" href="http://bowvalleysup.ca/Rentals/" target="_blank">Bow Valley SUP</a>? Discover more of Banff National Park off the beaten path. Driving west from Banff on Highway 1, take the exit to the <a href="/icefields-parkway">Icefields Parkway/93 North</a>. About 20 minutes down the road, turn off at Bow Lake to enjoy a paddle with a view of the Bow Glacier. This is the water that feeds into the Bow River. </p>
<p>Extend your stay on the Icefields Parkway and explore more of the park, take your time and see another hidden gem just a short drive north: Waterfowl Lakes. With views this stunning, you may want to broaden your stay and make it an overnight trip. <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/camping" target="_blank">Parks Canada camping</a> is available on a first come first served basis, so why not soak in the sunset from your boat on the lake. </p>

<p>Plan ahead and book <a href="/accommodation">accommodation </a>to make it a long stay in the park. With so many lakes to explore by canoe, kayak, and SUP, there are extensive ways to build your itinerary. </p>
<p><a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/canotage-canoeing#safety" target="_blank">Safety </a>is key when enjoying the lakes and rivers Banff National Park has to offer. Take a few minutes to <a href="https://tc.canada.ca/en/marine-transportation/marine-safety/mandatory-safety-equipment">review the equipment you need</a> to stay safe by boat. </p>
<p>If you're bringing your own floating device, make sure you check <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/nautiques-sports/nettoyez-videz-sechez-clean-drain-dry" target="_blank">Parks Canada Rules and Regulations</a> and download a self-certification permit.</p>
