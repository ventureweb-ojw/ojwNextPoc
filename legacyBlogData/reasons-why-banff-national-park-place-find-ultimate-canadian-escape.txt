<div><p>If you type “Canada travel” into Google image search, guess what you’ll find? Images of the stunning, blue-green lakes and snowcapped peaks of Banff National Park. People all over the world envision Banff and Lake Louise when they think of Canada – a destination that inspires the ultimate Canadian holiday experience. </p>
<h4>1. Visit Canada’s first national park </h4>
<p>It all started in 1883, when three Canadian Pacific Railway workers stumbled upon an exquisite hot spring cave on Sulphur Mountain. They tried to claim ownership of the springs, sparking a chain of events that led to the formation of Banff National Park in 1885 – the first reserve of natural beauty in Canada.   </p>
<h4>2. Sip on spirits from the world’s only distillery located in a national park </h4>
<p><a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Park Distillery Restaurant &amp; Bar" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2671" href="http://parkdistillery.com" target="_blank">Park Distillery</a> uses glacier fresh water to ensure its small-batch gin, vodka and rye divulge the true flavours of the mountains. Stop by to sample the proprietary maple rye, bird’s eye chili vodka or alpine dry gin, but don’t leave without trying Shaft on Draft; a coffee-laced delight that locals were drinking long before espresso martinis were a thing.  </p>
<h4>3. Explore First Nations heritage at the Buffalo Nations Luxton Museum </h4>
<p>Dive into the world of the Buffalo Nations people, who once moved their camp seasonally through the stunning landscapes of Banff National Park, following the great buffalo herds. From richly ornamented regalia to unique hunting equipment, explore a mesmerizing cultural celebration of Banff and Lake Louise.   </p>
<h4>4. Embrace Alberta’s cowboy spirit on a trail ride </h4>
<p>Saddle up partner, you’re headed out on a horseback adventure through the Canadian Rocky Mountains. <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Trail Riders" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2205" href="http://bll.fyi/pyft" target="_blank">Banff Trail Riders</a> can take customize your experience, taking you on a leisurely carriage ride through town or whisking you into the wild on a multi-day horseback campout or lodge stay.  </p>
<h4>5. Jump on the chance to spot Canada’s favourite wildlife </h4>
<p>If you visit at the right time of year it’s highly likely you’ll spot a bear in Banff National Park, but these furry favourites are just the beginning. Elk and deer are a common sighting on the streets of Banff and Lake Louise, and mountain goats and big horned sheep are often seen on the roads to the ski resorts.  </p>
<p>You may even be lucky enough to spot a moose, or hear the slap of a beaver’s tail on the waters of the Bow River. Remember to keep your distance and help keep our wildlife wild. </p>
<h4>6. Get up close and personal with some of Canada’s most beautiful water </h4>
<p>We’ve all experienced FOMO thanks to that iconic image of the red canoe paddling across aqua coloured waters, but in Banff National Park it’s easy to put yourself in the picture. Rent a canoe from the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="92.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Canoe Club" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2528" href="https://bll.fyi/yb11" target="_blank">Banff Canoe Club</a> and paddle along Banff’s Bow River, launch a SUP onto the glowing waters of Bow Lake, or dare yourself to take a polar bear plunge into Two Jack.  </p>

<h4>8. Visit Canada’s highest alpine community </h4>
<p>Feel like getting high? Wander through the hamlet of Lake Louise, the most elevated community in Canada. <h4>9. Sample a top-notch Caesar </h4>
<p>There’s nothing like the celery-salted perfection of a well-made Caesar, which is somehow enhanced by the addition of mountain views. Try the Bacon Caesar at <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="32.9500" data-gtm_label="The Lake Louise Gondola" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2238" href="https://www.skilouise.com/dining/on-mountain-dining/whitehorn-bistro" target="_blank">Whitehorn Bistro</a> at the Lake Louise Ski Resort and Summer Gondola. Whether it is summer or winter, you’ll be treated to views as iconic as your tasty Canadian cocktail.  </p>
<h4>10. Eat Canadian food at its finest </h4>
<p>Whether it’s squeaky poutine at Banff Poutine, delectable desserts from <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Beavertails" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Fast Food" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2620" href="http://www.beavertailsinc.com/" target="_blank">Beavertails </a>or prime Alberta Beef at <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Chuck's Steakhouse Ltd." data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2750" href="http://www.chuckssteakhouse.ca/" target="_blank">Chuck’s Steak House</a>, you can eat your fill of Canadian favourites in Banff National Park. For a true taste of farm-to-table freshness, dine at <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="60.0000" data-gtm_label="Farm &amp; Fire" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2022" href="https://www.banffjaspercollection.com/dining/farm-fire/" target="_blank">Farm &amp; Fire</a>, a new restaurant with a focus on locally-sourced flavours.   </p>
<h4>11. Kick back on rooftop patios with views of the Rockies </h4>
<p>Kick back on one of Banff’s rooftop patios, sip a locally-brewed beer, and marvel at the towering peaks of the Canadian Rocky Mountains. Local favourites include the patio at Mexican-themed <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Magpie &amp; Stump and Cantina" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2252" href="http://www.magpieandstump.ca" target="_blank">Magpie and Stump</a>, the beautifully renovated rooftop at <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Rose &amp; Crown" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2038" href="http://www.roseandcrown.ca" target="_blank">Rose and Crown</a>, and the festive space atop the <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Elk &amp; Oarsman Restaurant &amp; Sports Bar" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2267" href="https://elkandoarsman.com/" target="_blank">Elk &amp; Oarsman</a>.  </p>
<h4>12. Relish locally-brewed beer </h4>
<p>There’s nothing like an ice-cold pint after a day in the mountains. You’ll find locally-brewed beer proudly featured on most Banff and Lake Louise menus, but head to <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Avenue Brew Pub" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="1989" href="http://www.banffavebrewingco.ca" target="_blank">Banff Ave Brewing Co</a>. for a comprehensive tasting experience.  </p>
<h4>13. All are invited to enjoy awe-inspiring moments </h4>
<p>You don’t have to be an extreme adventurer to enjoy Banff National Park. The powerful beauty of this place is easy to experience, whether you’re gaping at Castle Mountain on a scenic drive, taking an easy paddle on blue-green waters, or browsing the shops and galleries of Bear Street.  </p>

<h4>14. Experience all-season experiences that suit an all-weather country </h4>
<p>In Canada we don’t let the weather stop us from having a good time - in fact, we use it to our advantage. In Banff and Lake Louise we love the winter and its invitation to snowy adventures, but fall is a locals’ favourite thanks to the flaming gold of the larch trees. Spring is perfect for early-season hiking and lingering après-ski sessions, while summer invites you to play on the gorgeous waters of the glacial-fed lakes.  </p>
<h4>15. Golf at one of the most scenic courses in the world </h4>
<p><a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Springs Golf Course" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2475" href="http://www.fairmontgolf.com/banffsprings" target="_blank">The Fairmont Banff Springs Golf Course</a> is commonly listed among the most beautiful golf courses in the world. As you tee off towards Mount Rundle or drive your cart beside the rushing Bow River you can’t help but agree. Wrap up your day on this designer golf course with a drink on the balcony of the <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Waldhaus Pub &amp; Patio" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2483" href="http://www.fairmont.com/banff-springs/dining/waldhauspub/?genid=waldhauspubandpatio_bsh_blltdining&amp;utm_source=waldhauspubandpatio&amp;utm_medium=paid_referrer&amp;utm_content=bsh&amp;utm_campaign=blltdining" target="_blank">Waldhaus Pub &amp; Biergarten</a>.   </p>
<h4>16. Get your adrenaline or relaxation fix on Mt. Norquay </h4>
<p>Adrenaline seekers will love the ladders and suspension bridges of <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="250.0000" data-gtm_label="Mt Norquay's Via Ferrata" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2609" href="https://bll.fyi/2fyw" target="_blank">Mt. Norquay’s Via Ferrata</a>, while those more inclined to relaxation will love a stop at Cliffhouse Bistro, originally a 1950s teahouse. Savour outstanding Canadian cuisine and unbeatable views over Banff National Park. </p>

<h4>17. Visit the Castle in the Rockies </h4>
<p>The spires of the <a data-gtm_category="Accommodation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Fairmont Banff Springs" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Hotels, Hostels &amp; Inns" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2473" href="http://www.fairmont.com/banff-springs" target="_blank">Fairmont Banff Springs</a> rise above the tops of the evergreens like those of a fairytale castle. You can book a suite at this luxurious hotel, or visit for the day to take a foodie tour, explore the haunted staircase and browse a range of shops and art galleries.  </p>
<h4>18. Marvel at the phenomenon of alpenglow </h4>
<p>Catch the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="49.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Gondola" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2004" href="https://bll.fyi/lhpm" target="_blank">Banff Gondola</a> to the summit of Sulphur Mountain for the ultimate sunset experience. In summer you’ll be treated to live music as you marvel at the mountain views, but the wonder of alpenglow is yours to enjoy all year round. Check sunset times before you visit, as it can vary from 4:30 p.m. to 10:30 p.m. throughout the year.   </p>
<h4>19. Immerse yourself in Canadian Rocky Mountain heritage at a local museum </h4>
<p>Learn about the fascinating history and unique culture of the Canadian Rocky Mountains at the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Whyte Museum of the Canadian Rockies" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Arts, Culture &amp; Heritage" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2459" href="http://www.whyte.org" target="_blank">Whyte Museum</a>. Opening for summer 2020 on July 1, the Whyte Museum is more than just a collection; it’s a place that inspires an exchange of knowledge and ideas through exhibitions and special programs.  </p>
<h4>20. Ski at one of three ski resorts uniquely located within a national park</h4>
<p>Hit the slopes at one of three, world-class ski resorts in Banff National Park. <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="65.0000" data-gtm_label="Mt. Norquay" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2098" href="https://bll.fyi/9jrv" target="_blank">Mount Norquay</a> is just a 15-minute drive from the town of Banff, a great stop for families that boasts terrific views over the valley. <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="105.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Sunshine Village" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2143" href="https://bll.fyi/1i95" target="_blank">Banff Sunshine Village Ski Resort</a> is famous for the quality of its champagne powder snow, which falls in such high quantities that the resort has no need for man-made flakes. In 2019 the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="100.0000" data-gtm_label="The Lake Louise Ski Resort" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2603" href="https://bll.fyi/r495" target="_blank">Lake Louise Ski Resort</a> was voted Canada’s Best Ski Resort in the renowned World Ski Awards, the sixth time the family-owned resort has been awarded for its awesome terrain and facilities. </p>
