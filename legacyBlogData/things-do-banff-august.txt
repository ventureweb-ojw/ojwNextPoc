<div><p>With summer in full swing, Banff and Lake Louise are full of life in August. Stunning mountain vistas, vibrant turquoise lakes that glisten in the warm, summer sun, greater chances of spotting wildlife, and a summer event series, make August one of the most popular times to visit!</p>
<p>If you're looking for ideas to make the most of your summer trip to Banff National Park, check out these great options.</p>
<p><strong>Local's Tip:</strong> The easiest way to explore Banff National Park in August is to plan ahead and take transit. You can find all transit and shuttle options <a href="/explorethepark">here</a>.</p>
<h4 dir="ltr">Get out on the water!</h4>
<p dir="ltr">Banff National Park is famous for its glimmering lakes and rushing rivers – why not take your adventures out on the water? Rent a canoe, kayak, or SUP and explore the numerous paddling locations throughout the area. You can find local rental companies and plenty of inspiration for locations on our <a href="/paddling">paddling page</a>. Want some adrenaline? Book a <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="120.0000" data-gtm_label="Discover Banff Tours" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2380" href="https://www.banfftours.com/things-to-do/banff-whitewater-rafting/" target="_blank">whitewater rafting</a> excursion. If sitting back and relaxing is more your speed, the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="60.0000" data-gtm_label="Lake Minnewanka Cruise" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2384" href="https://bll.fyi/q9r5?utm_source=BLL&amp;utm_medium=PkgListings&amp;utm_campaign=BLL_Web13" target="_blank">Lake Minnewanka Cruise</a> lets you soak up stunning views, alongside a primer of the area’s history, geology, and wildlife. </p>

<h4 dir="ltr">Hike to a teahouse! </h4>
<p dir="ltr">Vistas aren’t the only thing waiting at the end of your trek. In Banff National Park, two <a href="/blog/tea-house-hikes-banff-national-park">tea houses</a> are nestled in the mountains accessible from trails starting at Lake Louise. Hike up about 400 metres and you’ll arrive at the stunning <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Lake Agnes Tea House" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Coffee Houses &amp; Tea Rooms" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2236" href="http://www.lakeagnesteahouse.com" target="_blank">Lake Agnes</a>, where you’ll find a tea house that’s more than a century old. <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Plain of Six Glaciers Tea House" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Coffee Houses &amp; Tea Rooms" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2132" href="http://www.p6teahouse.com" target="_blank">The Plain of Six Glaciers</a> hike leaves from the back of Lake Louise, leading you to a Swiss-built tea house. Don’t forget your cash – these teahouses don’t accept credit cards. </p>

<h4 dir="ltr">Take a Gondola Ride to Adventure</h4>
<p dir="ltr">Starting at the bottom of a mountain and hiking your way up is one option. Or take a <a href="/gondolas">gondola</a> to access harder-to-reach, scenic destinations. Try the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="32.9500" data-gtm_label="The Lake Louise Gondola" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2238" href="http://bll.fyi/pb82" target="_blank">Lake Louise Gondola</a>, and hike to the Ptarmigan Valley or Kicking Horse viewpoints. You could also venture up the open chairlift at Mt. Norquay where you will find the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="250.0000" data-gtm_label="Mt Norquay's Via Ferrata" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2609" href="https://bll.fyi/2fyw" target="_blank">Via Ferrata</a> - a guided assisted climbing experience. </p>

<h4 dir="ltr">Grab a snack and enjoy the scenery</h4>
<p dir="ltr">You don’t have to break a sweat to enjoy the mountains. Take your pick of <a href="/blog/9-patios-you-need-try-summer-banff-lake-louise">patios with stunning views</a> and delicious food and drinks, like Banff Centre’s <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Maclab Bistro" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2608" href="https://www.banffcentre.ca/maclab-bistro" target="_blank">MacLab Bistro</a> on Tunnel Mountain, <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Park Distillery Restaurant &amp; Bar" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2671" href="http://parkdistillery.com" target="_blank">Park Distillery </a>in downtown Banff, or <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Juniper Hotel Bistro" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Bar &amp; Restaurant" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2753" href="https://bll.fyi/eiov" target="_blank">Juniper Bistro</a> at the base of Mt Norquay. Looking for a more rustic experience? Cascade Ponds, on the Lake Minnewanka Scenic Drive, offer a series of benches and campfire pits for your <a href="/blog/perfecting-classic-mountain-picnic-banff-national-park">perfect picnic</a>. </p>

<h4 dir="ltr">Drive the Icefield Parkway</h4>
<p dir="ltr">The <a href="/icefields-parkway">Icefield Parkway</a> is a scenic highway known for its glacier-topped mountains. A thirty-minute drive north, you’ll find Bow Lake – the source of the Bow River. You’ll want to stop at Peyto Lake next. This beauty isn’t visible from the road, but a short, steep hike up a paved path will give you a sweeping view of the vibrant blue lake and seemingly endless mountain ranges. </p>
<p dir="ltr">Make your next stop Mistaya Canyon and marvel at its depth as the Mistaya River plunges through it. Want to see a glacier up close? Drive an hour further north and you’ll hit the Athabasca Glacier, where you can park your car and hike up to the foot of the glacier, or book a trip to explore it close up with the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="80.0000" data-gtm_label="Columbia Icefield Glacier Adventure" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2359" href="https://bll.fyi/4wl6?utm_source=BLL&amp;utm_medium=PkgListings&amp;utm_campaign=BLL_Web13" target="_blank">Columbia Icefield Adventure</a>. For another perspective, the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="32.0000" data-gtm_label="Columbia Icefield Skywalk" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2540" href="https://padlock.link/rlfx" target="_blank">Glacier Skywalk</a> lets you stroll on a glass viewing platform to marvel at the glaciers below. </p>

<h4 dir="ltr">Hanging out in the town of Banff</h4>
