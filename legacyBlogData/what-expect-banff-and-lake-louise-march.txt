<div><p>March brings the first day of spring, and in a mountain town, that means only one thing: spring skiing! </p>
<p><meta charset="utf-8"/></p>
<p dir="ltr">If you’ve never skied in the Rockies between March and May, then you’re in for a treat! The days are mild, the snow is still falling, and the après patios are full of happy, sun-warmed faces.</p>
<p dir="ltr">Are you ready to see how the Canadians take on the last days of winter? </p>
<p dir="ltr">Here’s what you can expect to see, do, and experience in Banff and Lake Louise in March. </p>

<h4 dir="ltr">The Weather </h4>
<p dir="ltr">December, January, and February are typical for an Albertan winter. The months are cold and generally filled with a good dumping of snow. But while the rest of the province starts to warm their chilled wind-blown prairie bones, Banff and Lake Louise challenge the mountains to a few more months of fun into March, April, and May. </p>
<p dir="ltr">This means that you might experience spring-like weather in town, but up in the mountains, you get a milder winter, so you can play in the snow all day long.  </p>
<p dir="ltr">Banff Daytime 4°C (24.8°F) </p>
<p dir="ltr">Banff Overnight -8°C (17.6°F) </p>
<p dir="ltr">Lake Louise Daytime 3°C (37.4°F)</p>
<p dir="ltr">Lake Louise Overnight -13°C (8.6°F) </p>

<h4 dir="ltr">What to Pack </h4>
<p dir="ltr">You’re going to need to make use of every square inch of your suitcase for your trip in March. As the lower elevations begin to rise in temperature, the higher elevations can remain relatively cool. So if you plan on doing any exploring beyond the townsites, you’ll want to bring a variety of clothing options. </p>
<p dir="ltr">Pack a winter coat, toque, mitts, scarf, snow pants, and winter boots for out-of-town travels. When possible, pick clothing that is water-resistant and moisture-wicking.</p>
<p dir="ltr">On the mountain, you’ll want to dress a little lighter than our deep-set winter months since you’ll warm up quickly and want to remove layers. Don’t forget the wind layers, though. The chairlifts and summits can carry a chilly breeze: outer shells and a Buff for your ears should do! </p>
<p dir="ltr">In town, you’ll get a mix of balmy, sunny days and some that are still quite nippy. For this reason, you’ll want layers but can generally get away with short jaunts from your hotel to the restaurants with a winter jacket and boots. </p>
<p dir="ltr">Alberta is one of the sunniest places in Canada, so don’t forget to bring sunscreen.</p>

<h4 dir="ltr">What to See and Do</h4>
<p dir="ltr">As many other parts of Canada thaw out – Banff and Lake Louise remain go-to destinations for “winter” activities because of our extended season.</p>
<p dir="ltr">If you haven’t guessed already, March is great for skiing and snowboarding, but there are options beyond our <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="2099.0000" data-gtm_label="SkiBig3" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2055" href="https://padlock.link/93y9" target="_blank">SkiBig3 </a>resorts. Book a heli-ski trip with <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="CMH Heli-Skiing" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Ski &amp; Snowboard" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2357" href="https://www.cmhheli.com/" target="_blank">CMH</a> to up the ante before the season ends. They’ll take you to ski lines untraced where only the mountain goats roam.</p>
<p dir="ltr">March also holds excellent <a href="/cross-country-skiing">cross-country skiing</a>, <a href="/snowshoeing">snowshoeing</a>, and <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/etat-sentiers-trail-conditions" target="_blank">winter hiking</a> conditions. As the snow melts in the sunshine, it can freeze overnight, so be prepared for ice with good boots, cleats, and walking poles. </p>
