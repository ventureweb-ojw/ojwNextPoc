<div><p>Banff National Park is Alive, and as such, it is constantly growing and changing. As both Banff and Lake Louise continue to welcome back visitors from near and far, it is essential to have the most up-to-date information to ensure trip planning is as smooth as possible. Here are 7 ways Banff and Lake Louise have changed in the past year.</p>
<h4>1. New Restaurants</h4>
<p>Banff is known for its wide array of culinary options and unique food experiences. This year the already bustling food scene has expanded with even more distinctive offers such as a Japanese Sushi &amp; Karaoke Bar, new cozy restaurants featuring local cuisine, and even a brewery with a retractable roof for stargazing. You can find all the new and exciting restaurants that opened this year <a href="/blog/your-guide-new-banff-restaurants">here</a>.</p>

<h4>2. New Accommodation</h4>
<p>From cozy cabins to rooftop hot tubs (and everything in between), Banff National Park is known for an array of mountain-themed accommodation. This year, there are some new accommodation additions, including the <a href="https://www.peaksbanff.com/">Peaks Hotel &amp; Suites</a>, located less than a minute walk from downtown Banff. Many favourites such as <a data-gtm_category="Accommodation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Aspen Lodge" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Hotels, Hostels &amp; Inns" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="1988" href="http://www.banffaspenlodge.com">Banff Aspen Lodge</a> and the <a data-gtm_category="Accommodation" data-gtm_event_value="99.0000" data-gtm_label="The Dorothy Motel" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Hotels, Hostels &amp; Inns" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2444" href="http://www.dorothymotel.com">Dorothy Motel</a> (formerly Bumpers Inn) have also reopened as they have recently been beautified and updated for your comfort.</p>

Adventure seekers are encouraged to join a <a href="/biking/e-biking">guided e-bike</a> tour, <a href="/blog/outdoor-classroom-guided-hikes-banff-and-lake-louise">guided backcountry hike</a>, or <a href="/water-activities/stand-up-paddleboard">guided SUP tour</a> to help you experience the fun in an accessible way. <h4>4. New Retail Stores</h4>
<p>Mountain souvenirs are the best souvenirs! Now you are even more spoiled for choice with an array of new shops to choose from. Sort through a selection of hats and accessories at <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="BRIMS AND THINGS" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Jewelry &amp; Accessories" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2945" href="">Brims &amp; Things</a> along Banff Avenue. Or perhaps you’d prefer searching for comfy sweaters and local artwork at <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Big Bear Trading Co." data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Souvenir" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2969" href="">Big Bear Trading Co</a>. The sweet tooth in your group can also indulge in Banff’s newest dessert haven, <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Rocky Mountain Chocolate Factory" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2956" href="https://rockychoc.com/">Rocky Mountain Chocolate Factory</a>. Shopping for souvenirs is even easier with the pedestrian zone along Banff Avenue and the newly renovated, pedestrian-friendly <a href="/blog/bear-street-beat-exploring-one-banff%E2%80%99s-most-vibrant-streets">Bear Street</a>.</p>
<h4>5. New Cycling Opportunities</h4>
<p>Cycling enthusiasts are invited to get out on the open road now more than ever in Banff National Park. Throughout the summer months, the Bow Valley Parkway (Highway 1A) has been closed to vehicle access from the Fireside Day Use Area to Johnston Canyon to allow cyclists the space to explore. This <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/cyclisme-biking/saisonintermediaire-shoulderseason">scenic ride</a> offers stunning views, hiking opportunities, and even the potential to spot some of our wild neighbours. You also have plenty of <a href="/biking">bike rental options</a>, including e-bikes, in both Banff and Lake Louise. When you return to the Banff Townsite, you can also swing by the <a href="https://banff.ca/100/Cycling">Bike Valet</a> located on Banff Avenue. Park your bike in the valet for free as you shop, dine, and relax in town. After all that cycling, you have earned a break!</p>

<h4>6. New Parking Lots and Parking Regulations</h4>
<p>As parking can be limited, we invite you to explore Banff National Park car-free whenever possible. To help avoid congestion, visitors are encouraged to park at the <a href="https://banff.ca/1104/Train-Station-Public-Parking">Banff Train Station</a> – offering 500 stalls and free parking with a time limit of 9 hours. From here, it is a short 10-minute walk to downtown, or you can also take advantage of the <a href="https://roamtransit.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/21-0513-Schedule_TrainStation.pdf">free shuttle</a> (offered Friday – Sunday) from the parking lot.</p>
<p>Additionally, paid parking has now been implemented at the Lake Louise lakeshore. Therefore, we recommend using <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/visit/parkbus/louise">public transportation</a>, including Parks Canada shuttles or Roam Public Transit for ease of access to Lake Louise.</p>

<h4>7. Watercraft Procedures from Parks Canada</h4>
<p>An important initiative within the national park is to protect our water from harmful aquatic invasive species. These stunning turquoise lakes are extraordinary, and we recognize the responsibility we have to protect them for years to come. Therefore, anyone launching watercraft such as canoes, kayaks, paddleboards, and inflatables requires a <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/nautiques-sports/nettoyez-videz-sechez-clean-drain-dry">self-certification permit</a>. It is important to read the conditions listed on the form (i.e. Drying your watercraft for a minimum of 48 hours after being used) and to ensure the survey is completed each time any water-related gear is used within Banff National Park.</p>
