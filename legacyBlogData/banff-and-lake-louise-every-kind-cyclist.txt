<div><p>Balanced on two wheels, the force of your own feet propels you forward as you bend the air around you.</p>
<p>The wind combs through the wisps of your hair poking out from below your helmet, the trees blur as you whip past, and whatever allures you—the thrill of the ride or a pint at the finish line—beckons you onward.</p>
<p>This is what it is like to <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Town Hall" data-gtm_no_track="Y" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2605" href="http://www.banffparking.ca" target="_blank">park your car</a> and explore Banff and Lake Louise by bicycle or e-bike. </p>
<h4>Sightseeing</h4>
<p>Does a full day of exploring and not waiting in the car sound too good to be true? What about if you add in some scenery and a basket filled with fresh-out-of-the-oven rolls and deserts for a picnic? Dreams really do come true but to make it happen, you’ll want to travel by bicycle. </p>
<p>Banff is easier to get around on a bike rather than a car since there are plenty of paved trail systems and only a few main arteries for vehicles. </p>
<p>So grab your bike, pick up the fixings for a picnic at a <a href="/dining-nightlife">local bakery or café</a> in town and enjoy a leisurely cycle on the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Town Hall" data-gtm_no_track="Y" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2605" href="https://banff.ca/650/Fenland-Trail" target="_blank">Fenland Trail</a> towards <a href="/vermilion-lakes">Vermillion Lakes</a>. Find a little more seclusion, and perhaps an opportunity to see some elk early in the morning, when you cycle the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Cycle" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2543" href="https://www.banffcycle.com/golf-course-loop" target="_blank">Banff Springs Golf Club loop</a>. </p>
<p>Rather put yourself in expert hands? Join <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="White Mountain Adventures" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2458" href="http://www.whitemountainadventures.com" target="_blank">White Mountain Adventures</a> or <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Cycle" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2543" href="http://www.banffcycle.com" target="_blank">Banff Cycle</a> for an e-bike townsite tour. <h4>Road Biking </h4>
<p>Voted some of the most scenic roads in the world, Banff and Lake Louise views will not disappoint—and you can actually enjoy them on an open-air bike ride. </p>
<p>Clip into your pedals and warm up with a quick trip to Canmore on the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/cyclismeroute-roadcycling/Heritage-Legacy" target="_blank">Legacy Trail</a> (22.3 km one way). Should that be enough for one day, hop on the <a href="http://bll.fyi/o728">Roam bus</a> for a ride back to Banff; just be prepared that there might not be enough room for bicycles and you may need to pedal back. Private transfers can also be arranged when you book with a tour guide like <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="White Mountain Adventures" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2458" href="http://www.whitemountainadventures.com" target="_blank">White Mountain Adventures</a>.</p>

<p>Ready to really get your kilometres in? Take the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/cyclismeroute-roadcycling/Banff#bow" target="_blank">Bow Valley Parkway</a> from Castle Junction to <a href="/johnston-canyon">Johnston Canyon</a> (12.8 km return) for a shorter trip, from <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Town Hall" data-gtm_no_track="Y" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2605" href="https://banff.ca/1104/Train-Station-Public-Parking" target="_blank">Banff Train Station Public Parking</a> to Johnston Canyon (49 km return) for a longer trip, or go all the way to<a href="/lake-louise"> Lake Louise</a> (114.8 km return) for a true test of your long-distance stamina!</p>
<p>If you're lacking confidence or just lazy, but still want this experience, <a href="/biking">try an e-bike</a>. You'll be leading the pack and breezing up the hills but without the huffing and puffing.</p>
<p><a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/visit/installations-facilities/fermetures-closures" target="_blank">Parks Canada</a> has made your trip easier by closing the eastern section of the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/cyclismeroute-roadcycling/Banff#bow" target="_blank">Bow Valley Parkway</a>, from Fireside day-use area to Castle Junction, to vehicles. They’ve done the same for the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/cyclismeroute-roadcycling/Banff#minnewanka" target="_blank">Minnewanka Loop</a> from May 1 - 20 between Monday - Thursday, meaning there are more car-free routes to explore in 2021!</p>
<p>However, if these suggestions have you laughing and these trips seem more like a simple warm-up—you may be ready to sink your teeth into something a little more hardcore. If so, book a guided trip with <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Cycle" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2543" href="https://www.banffcycle.com/custom-cycling-tours" target="_blank">Banff Cycle</a> to do a multi-day ride up the <a href="/icefields-parkway">Icefields Parkway</a> to Jasper (287 km one way).</p>
<h4>Mountain Biking </h4>
<p>Should the scenery not be appealing enough and you’re looking for a little more oomph—specifically elevation—get your heart pumping with some off-road (but on-trail) mountain biking. </p>
<p>In Banff, pin it on one of these three trails <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/velomontagne-mountainbiking/Banff#Tunnelloop" target="_blank">Tunnel Campground Loop</a>, <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/velomontagne-mountainbiking/Banff#Bankhead" target="_blank">Cascades Ponds to Bankhead</a>, or <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/velomontagne-mountainbiking/Banff#goat%20creek" target="_blank">Goat Creek Trail towards Canmore</a>.</p>
<p>In Lake Louise, take a leisurely ride on the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/velomontagne-mountainbiking/LakeLouise#bow%20river" target="_blank">Bow River Loop</a> or find your flow on a more demanding trail like <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/velomontagne-mountainbiking/LakeLouise#pipestone" target="_blank">Pipestone</a>, <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/velomontagne-mountainbiking/LakeLouise#ross" target="_blank">Ross Lake</a>, or <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/velomontagne-mountainbiking/LakeLouise#moraine" target="_blank">Moraine Lake Highline</a>—just keep an eye out for grizzlies who feed on nearby buffalo berries. </p>
<p>If you need a confidence boost or want to improve your skills in mountain biking, reach out to <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="89.0000" data-gtm_label="Bikescape Inc." data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2965" href="https://bikescape.ca/en/" target="_blank">Bikescape</a>. They provide the perfect opportunity for most ages and abilities to get out there, ride safely, and have fun.</p>
