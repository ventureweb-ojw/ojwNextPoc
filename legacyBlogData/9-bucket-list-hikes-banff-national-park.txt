<div><p>Banff National Park. For many, these words conjure up images of breathtaking scenery, turquoise-blue lakes, and a panoramic sea of peaks, of wildflowers dancing in the breeze, and wildlife minding their young next to flowing creeks. For many more that same scene includes endless trails just beckoning hikers to engross themselves in the landscape to gain those lofty views on their own two feet. </p>
<p>With over 1,600 kilometres (994 miles) of trails, Banff National Park offers adventurers some of the best hiking on the planet, whether it’s an easy hike to incredible vistas or a more strenuous trek deep in the backcountry. Choosing just nine bucket list hikes is about as big a task as climbing the biggest peaks in the Park, but here’s a start: </p>
<h5>Please note: before setting out on your adventures, be sure to check the latest trail conditions with <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking" target="_blank">Parks Canada</a>.</h5>
<h4><b>1. Cory Pass – Mt. Edith Circuit</b></h4>
<p>The <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff#Cory" target="_blank">hike to Cory Pass</a> requires some serious leg power, but the view from the pass is one of the most spectacular and rewarding in all of Banff National Park. The sheer south face of Mt. Louis surprises hikers as they crest the pass – a great place to sit and enjoy a packed lunch. Continue the circuit upon reaching the pass by descending down the other side and around Mt. Edith (a bit easier on the legs), or return the way you came. </p>
<p>Distance: 13 km (8 mi.) loop<br/>
Elevation gain: 1000 m (3280 ft)<br/>
Time required: 5-6 hours<br/>
Difficulty: Challenging</p>

<h4><b>2. Cascade Amphitheatre </b></h4>
<p>This hike begins at the Mt. Norquay ski area, crosses a bridge over Forty Mile Creek, then zigzags its way through the dense forest to the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/tcond/cond_e.asp?oPark=100092" target="_blank">Cascade Amphitheatre</a>. As you emerge into the amphitheatre, impressively enclosed by limestone cliffs, keep your eyes out for wildflowers. Adventurous hikers can make a full day trip of it and, with adequate preparation, tackle the summit of <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff" target="_blank">Cascade Mountain</a>. </p>
<p>Distance: 13.2 km (8 mi.) return trip<br/>
Elevation gain: 640 m (2099 ft)<br/>
Time required: 4-6 hours <br/>
Difficulty: Moderate</p>

<h4><b>3. Bourgeau Lake &amp; Harvey Pass</b></h4>
<p><a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/tcond/cond_e.asp?oPark=100092" target="_blank">Bourgeau Lake and the Harvey Pass</a> area above it deserve a full day’s trip. The trail climbs steeply through a forest of lodgepole and spruce before opening out into meadows that lead to the shores of Bourgeau Lake. From there, continue 2.2 km (310 m up) past several smaller bodies of water to the lake at <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff" target="_blank">Harvey Pass</a> for impressive views of Mt. Assiniboine, “The Matterhorn of the Rockies.”</p>
<p>Distance: 15 km (9.3 mi.) return trip<br/>
Elevation gain: 725 m (2378 ft)<br/>
Time required:  5-6 hours <br/>
Difficulty: Moderate</p>

<h4><b>4. Lake Agnes Teahouse/Big Beehive</b></h4>
<p>The hike to <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/tcond/cond_e.asp?oPark=100092" target="_blank">Lake Agnes</a> in the Lake Louise area and the historic teahouse on its shores is a relatively easy hike through an old-growth forest. A beautiful waterfall flows out of Lake Agnes just below the <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Lake Agnes Tea House" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Coffee Houses &amp; Tea Rooms" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2236" href="http://www.lakeagnesteahouse.com" target="_blank">Lake Agnes teahouse</a>, where you can enjoy a cup of tea and fresh piece of pie. For an added challenge, continue 1.6 km (1 mi.) around the far end of Lake Agnes and up switchbacks to the top of the Big Beehive for views of the Bow Valley and the turquoise-blue waters of <a href="/lake-louise">Lake Louise</a>.</p>
<p>Distance: 7.2 km (4.5 mi.) return trip<br/>
Elevation gain: 390 m (1279 ft)<br/>
Time required: 3-4 hours<br/>
Difficulty: Easy</p>

<h4><b>5. Plain of Six Glaciers</b></h4>
<p>By far one of the most accessible and scenic hikes in Banff National Park, the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/tcond/cond_e.asp?oPark=100092" target="_blank">Plain of Six Glaciers trail</a> offers expansive views of the impressive mountain features that surround Lake Louise, including Mt. Lefroy, Mt. Victoria, and the Victoria Glacier. After your steady hike up, enjoy your lunch at the plateau before continuing 1 km (0.6 mi.) to talus slopes that provide a view of Abbot Pass and the Abbot Pass Hut. Make sure you stop by the <a data-gtm_category="Food &amp; Beverage" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Plain of Six Glaciers Tea House" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Coffee Houses &amp; Tea Rooms" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2132" href="http://www.p6teahouse.com" target="_blank">Plain of Six Glaciers tea house</a> for a well deserved snack before heading back. </p>
<p>Distance: 13.8 km (8.6 mi.) return trip<br/>
Elevation gain: 380 m (1246 ft)<br/>
Time required: 4-6 hours<br/>
Difficulty: Easy</p>

<h4><b>6. Saddleback Pass/Mt. Fairview</b></h4>
<p>The hike to this gap between <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/lakelouise" target="_blank">Mt. Fairview</a> and <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/lakelouise" target="_blank">Saddle Peak</a> promises stunning views of the 11,000-foot Mt. Temple and, in autumn, the stunning gold shimmer of alpine larches. Scramble up Saddle Peak to enhance your views of Mt. Temple and Paradise Valley below. From Saddleback Pass, <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/tcond/cond_e.asp?oPark=100092" target="_blank">hike up Fairview Mountain</a> (an additional 2-hour return trip, 400m up) for a spectacular birds-eye view of Lake Louise. </p>
<p>Distance: 7.4 km (4.6 mi.) return trip<br/>
Elevation gain: 600 m (1968 ft)<br/>
Time required: 3-4 hours<br/>
Difficulty: Moderate</p>

<h4><b>7. Moraine Lake Shoreline/Rockpile</b></h4>
<p>The hike around the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/tcond/cond_e.asp?oPark=100092" target="_blank">Moraine Lake shoreline</a> is the easiest way to appreciate the beauty of this famous alpine lake. A flat, easy trail weaves its way through shoreline trees and offers extraordinary views of the Ten Peaks, a dramatic row of summits all over 10,000 feet. Hike up the Rockpile for an alternative view of these indigo waters and the nearby Tower of Babel.</p>
<p>Distance: 3 km (1.9 mi.) return trip<br/>
Elevation gain: 0 m<br/>
Time required: 1-2 hours<br/>
Difficulty: Easy</p>

<h4><b>8. Larch Valley/Sentinel Pass</b></h4>
<p>The hike into <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/tcond/cond_e.asp?oPark=100092" target="_blank">Larch Valley and Sentinel Pass</a> is a <i>must</i> for any hiker wanting to have an experience that closely resembles the backcountry of Banff National Park. This moderate hike offers remarkable views of the Ten Peaks and if you head up <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/lakelouise" target="_blank">Sentinel Pass</a>, the dreamlike vista of Paradise Valley (look for climbers on the Grand Sentinel!). As the name suggests, this valley is spectacular in autumn, when the larch needles turn to gold.  </p>
<p>Note: This hike requires a minimum group of four at certain times of the year. Check <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/visit/fermetures-closures" target="_blank">Parks Canada seasonal restrictions</a> prior to heading out. </p>
<p>Distance: 11.6 km (7.2 mi.) return trip<br/>
Elevation gain: 725 m (2378 ft)<br/>
Time required: 4-5 hours<br/>
Difficulty: Moderate</p>

<h4><b>9. Helen Lake/Dolomite Pass</b></h4>
<p>After a steady hike up through the forest, the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/tcond/cond_e.asp?oPark=100092" target="_blank">Helen Lake trail</a> emerges into an alpine wonderland that remains above tree line until you reach one of Banff’s best lunch spots: Helen Lake. During a few weeks in summer, the resident marmots are joined by a stunning collection of colourful wildflowers. To lengthen your day, continue along the trail into <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/93N" target="_blank">Dolomite Pass</a> and towards Katherine Lake, where you will be treated to a view of the impressive pyramid of Mt. Assiniboine.</p>
<p>Distance: 12 km (7.5 mi.) return trip<br/>
Elevation gain: 450 m (1476 ft)<br/>
Time required: 4 hours<br/>
Difficulty: Moderate</p>
