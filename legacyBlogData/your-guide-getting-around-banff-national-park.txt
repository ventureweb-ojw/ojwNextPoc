<h4>Getting around the Town of Banff</h4>
<h5><strong>Roam Transit</strong></h5>
<p>Did you know the Town of Banff has its own transit system?  The Roam bus will get you around town and to some of the top attractions, including the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="49.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Gondola" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sightseeing &amp; Tours" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2004" href="https://bll.fyi/lhpm" target="_blank">Banff Gondola</a>, <a data-gtm_category="Accommodation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Fairmont Banff Springs" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Hotels, Hostels &amp; Inns" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2473" href="http://www.fairmont.com/banff-springs" target="_blank">Fairmont Banff Springs Hotel</a> and the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="3.9000" data-gtm_label="Cave &amp; Basin National Historic Site" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Arts, Culture &amp; Heritage" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2496" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/cave" target="_blank">Cave and Basin National Historic Site</a>.</p>
<p>Want to wander further afield? Roam still has you covered. Roam Route 6 will take you out of downtown Banff along the <a href="/blog/explore-lake-minnewanka-loop">Minnewanka Scenic Drive</a>. Don’t forget to get off the bus and explore! With stops conveniently located at the magnificent <a href="/lake-minnewanka">Lake Minnewanka</a>, <a data-gtm_category="Accommodation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Two Jack Main Campground" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Campgrounds" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2572" href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/eng/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/camping/twojack1.aspx" target="_blank">Two Jack campgrounds</a>, and day use area. Bring your snacks for a picnic and stop at the Cascade Ponds to build a campfire in one of Parks Canada’s designated fire pits and take in the sweeping views.</p>
<p>Interested in taking in a few stops in one day? Use Roam as your shuttle for the day, and take unlimited rides for $5 (seniors 65+ and children/youth 13-18 $2.50). A single fare is only $2 (seniors 65+ and children/youth 13 -18 $1). Children under 6 years old ride for free.  </p>
<p>For a complete breakdown of routes and schedules, please visit <a href="http://roamtransit.com">Roam Transit</a>. </p>

<h5><strong>On Foot</strong></h5>
<p>Finally, you’ve arrived in Banff. What better way to stretch your legs and enjoy the fresh air than to explore the area and its attractions on foot? Banff National Park is vast, with many sights and hikes to explore, but you don’t need to go far to discover the beauty the park has to offer. Some of the most popular attractions, sights, and hikes are accessible right in town.</p>
<p>Looking for a hike or a walk you can get to on foot? Try Tunnel Mountain, the Hoodoo Trail, Sulphur Mountain, Marsh Loop, Sundance Canyon, or the Fenlands Trail, all accessible on foot within the town. For more inspiration, check out our blog <a href="/blog/exploring-banff-and-lake-louise-your-own-two-feet">Exploring Banff On Your Own Two Feet</a>. </p>
<h5><strong>Bicycle</strong></h5>
<p>Forget about parking and enjoy the freedom of your own schedule by bringing or renting <a href="/biking">bicycles</a>. Bike up Tunnel Mountain Drive and stop at the Hoodoo lookout, cycle the Sundance Canyon Access Road, or take in a view of Mount Rundle along Vermilion Lakes Road. With a bicycle, you can make your itinerary on the fly and see where the day takes you!</p>
<p>Don’t have a bike or travelling light? Rent in town from around $35/day. </p>

<h5><strong>Canoe, Kayak, or SUP</strong></h5>
<p>This is a bit of a stretch, but hear us out. OK, so you can’t explore the entire town, but if you pick up a SUP rental from <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="SkiBig3 Adventure Hub" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sporting goods &amp; Apparel" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2056" href="https://bll.fyi/fgq3" target="_blank">SkiBig3 Adventure Hub</a> or a canoe, kayak, or SUP rental at the <a data-gtm_category="Activities" data-gtm_event_value="50.0000" data-gtm_label="Banff Canoe Club" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Outdoor Recreation" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2528" href="https://www.banffcanoeclub.com" target="_blank">Banff Canoe Club</a>, you can explore the Bow River upstream. Within minutes you’ve left downtown Banff behind and have a completely different view of the park. Local tip: When the water levels are high enough early in the season, paddle from the Bow River to the Vermilion Lakes. Looking for more paddling inspiration, check out <a href="/blog/paddles-in-banff-national-park">8 Jaw-Dropping Paddles to Explore in Banff National Park</a>. </p>
<h4>Getting to and around Lake Louise</h4>
<h5>Roam Transit </h5>
<p>If you want to spend the day out of town, pack for a day exploring Lake Louise or Johnston Canyon. Take advantage of Roam services to Lake Louise (8X or 8S), and Johnston Canyon (8S or 9). <a href="https://roamtransit.com/reservations/">Reservations </a>are required when riding the 8S or 9 to Johnston Canyon and Lake Louise. From September, leave the car in Banff and take an express ride to Moraine Lake with Roam 10. </p>
<h5><strong>Parks Canada Shuttle Buses</strong></h5>
<p>From May 14 - October 11, leave the car behind and let Parks Canada shuttle you around Lake Louise. Board the Roam bus from Banff to Lake Louise and disembark at the Lake Louise Village. Transfer to the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/visit/parkbus/louise" target="_blank">Parks Canada shuttle system</a> to the Lake Louise Park and Ride to explore. Combine the Park &amp; Ride connector, Moraine Lake shuttle (from June 1) or Lake Louise Shuttle, and the Lake Connector (from June 1) to visit both Moraine Lake and Lake Louise in one day. You will need to <a href="/lake-louise-moraine-summer-shuttle">pre-book Parks Canada shuttles</a> from April 28, 2021.  </p>
<p>For the perfect car-free day in Lake Louise, check out '<a href="/blog/perfect-summer-day-lake-louise">How to spend a summer day in Lake Louise</a>'. </p>
<p>For a complete listing of all shuttles in Banff National Park, visit <a href="/explorethepark">www.explorethepark.ca</a>.</p>

<h4>Explore Banff by Car</h4>
<p>It’s fair to say that driving through Banff National Park gives you the most flexibility in terms of plotting out your route. Not all attractions are accessible by other modes of transportation, but limited parking is a downside of taking a car to popular locations.</p>
<p>Arriving at Calgary Airport? You can book a car rental there. Or, take the <a data-gtm_category="Transportation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Banff Airporter" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Shuttles &amp; Taxis" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="1986" href="http://www.banffairporter.com" target="_blank">Banff Airporter</a> or <a data-gtm_category="Transportation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Brewster Express" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Shuttles &amp; Taxis" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2809" href="http://brewsterexpress.com" target="_blank">Brewster Shuttle</a> bus from the airport to Banff and reserve a car at one of a number of rental companies right in downtown Banff:</p>

<li><a data-gtm_category="Transportation" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Avis Rent a Car" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Rentals" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="1976" href="https://www.avis.com/en/home" target="_blank">Avis</a></li>
