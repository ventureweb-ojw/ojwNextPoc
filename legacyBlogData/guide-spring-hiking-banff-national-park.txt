<div><p>Warmer weather, fewer people, fresh mountain air, and longer days. Spring hiking in Banff National Park is exciting and rejuvenating. Play it safe: hike to your abilities, pack the bear spray, and gather your layers. Thaw out the hiking boots and prepare, with these tips, for spring hiking in Banff National Park. </p>

<h4>Best time of day for spring hiking in Banff and Lake Louise </h4>
<p>Spring is one of the best times to visit Banff National Park. To get the most out of your adventures, explore trails early. Pack a headlamp and hike as the sun rises along with all the animals, or enjoy a late afternoon and sunset hike (weather dependant). Check with the expert <a href="/visitor-centres">visitor services team </a>for the best times for your chosen hike.  </p>
<h4>What to pack for spring hiking in Banff and Lake Louise </h4>
<p>Hiking in the spring is a blend of conditions. You won’t find green meadows quite yet, but you will encounter icy snow, compact snow, and mud. <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking" target="_blank">Be prepared</a> for all seasons and for changing weather. Here are some recommended items to pack for spring hiking.  </p>
<ul>
<li>Water  </li>
<li>Bear spray  </li>
<li>Sunscreen and sunglasses  </li>
<li>Traction cleats  </li>
<li>Hat and gloves  </li>
<li>Rain/wind jacket  </li>
<li>First aid kit  </li>
<li>Headlamp or flashlight with spare batteries  </li>
<li>Hiking poles  </li>
<li>Extra warm clothing in case of an emergency </li>
<li>Cell phone or satellite emergency communication device  </li>
<li>Camera </li>
</ul>

<h4>Be safe</h4>
<p>Spring weather can be unpredictable in the Canadian Rockies. Fluctuating temperatures can mean risky snow conditions. When picking your adventures, visit Parks Canada online to check <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/etat-sentiers-trail-conditions" target="_blank">trail conditions</a>. With wildlife waking up, as much as you might want to headbang to The Weekend or Neil Young, keep alert and the headphones out.  </p>
