<p>Exciting for some, sad for others, but winter hiking is coming to an end as the snow melts in the lower regions. However, the higher elevations are still out of reach with too much snow. Spring is the perfect time to warm up your mountain legs on some lower-elevation trails in Banff and Lake Louise.  To all those eager beavers out there, you don’t need to wait too long to lace up their hiking boots!</p>
<p>Here’s a short guide to snow-free spots when the mountains are still cloaked in white up high:</p>
<h4><b>1. Tunnel Mountain </b></h4>
<p>At 1,692 metres high, this little mountain is perfectly positioned for the best views of the Banff townsite and Mt. Rundle. A local favourite, <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff" target="_blank">Tunnel Mountain</a> is a moderate hike that climbs via switchbacks to a rounded summit – one of the most scenic lunch spots around.</p>
<p><i>Access: Trailhead/parking lot on St. Julien Road</i></p>
<p><i>Length: 4.8 km round-trip</i></p>

<h4><b>3. Marsh Loop</b></h4>
<p>Whether it’s early-season or summer at its best, birdwatchers will want to add the <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff" target="_blank">Marsh Loop</a> to their list. This short hike through a natural marshland (don’t worry – you’re mostly walking on boardwalks!) is one of the best places in Banff to watch birds, as well as butterflies and dragonflies.</p>
<p><i>Access: Near the Cave &amp; Basin National Historic Site, 2 km walk from downtown Banff.</i></p>
<p><i>Length: 2.8 km loop</i></p>
<h4><b>5. Fenland Trail</b></h4>
<p>This short, <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff" target="_blank">meandering trail</a> leads you through beautiful wetlands, old-growth forest and the banks of Echo and Forty-Mile creeks, and is home to resident wildlife, such as elk, deer and a variety of birds. Lengthen your walk by connecting this hike with a saunter along Banff’s Bow River trail or Vermilion Lakes Road.</p>
<p><i>Access: Small parking and picnic area near the Mt. Norquay exit as you enter the Town of Banff</i></p>
<p><i>Length: 2.1 km loop</i></p>
<h4><b>4. Sundance Canyon Trail</b></h4>
<p>This pleasant trail takes you along the Bow River, past incredible views of the Mt. Edith and the Sawback Range, and to the base of <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff" target="_blank">Sundance Canyon.</a> You’ll be sharing the trail with horses and bikes for the first while, but in the quiet of the canyon, you can take some time to explore on your own. You’ll see a 1.6 km trail that loops past the canyon and back to the Sundance Canyon Trail, but it may be too snowy for hiking.</p>
<p><i>Access: The Cave and Basin National Historic Site</i></p>
<p><i>Length: 3.7 km one-way to the canyon 1.6 km moderately difficult loop through a canyon</i></p>

<h4><b>6. Sulphur Mountain</b></h4>
<p>This easy trail switchbacks its way up <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff" target="_blank">Sulphur Mountain</a> and offers unparalleled views of the Bow Valley. Once you’re at the top, you can continue along the 1 km interpretive boardwalk to the Cosmic Ray Station and Sanson Peak. </p>
<p><strong>Note</strong>: For summer 2021, to ensure the health and safety of guests on the Banff Gondola, hikers will not be able to take the gondola down Sulphur Mountain or access the gondola building and rooftop viewing deck.</p>
<p><i>Access: From the Banff Upper Hot Springs car park.</i></p>
<p><i>Length: 11 km return</i></p>
<h4><b>7. Minnewanka Lakeside</b></h4>
<p>A walk around Banff’s largest lake is a great way to experience this beautiful corner of Banff National Park. This trail winds its way along the shores of <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff" target="_blank">Lake Minnewanka</a> – for over 17 kilometres, in fact – so you can walk as far as you like and come back the same way. </p>
<p><i>Access: Lake Minnewanka parking lot</i></p>
<p><i>Length: 17.1 km one-way (go as far as you like) </i></p>
<h4><b>2. Hoodoo Trail</b></h4>
<p>If you’re keen to see some of Banff’s most striking geological features, you should venture down <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff" target="_blank">The Hoodoo Trail</a>, a pleasant, forested trail by the Bow River. The trail is 4.6-km one-way, but you’ll be coming back the way you went, so you choose the length!</p>
<p><i>Access: Surprise Corner viewpoint parking lot on Tunnel Mountain Drive, or a 1.6 km walk from downtown Banff at Surprise Corner.</i></p>
<p><i>Length: 4.8 km one-way</i></p>

<h4><b>8. Johnson Lake</b></h4>
<p>This leisurely path circumnavigates <a data-gtm_category="Services" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Parks Canada" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Community &amp; Government" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2548" href="https://www.pc.gc.ca/en/pn-np/ab/banff/activ/randonee-hiking/banff" target="_blank">Johnson Lake</a> and provides wonderful views of the surrounding peaks, such as Cascade Mountain and Mt. Rundle. It is a fantastic way to spend part of the day at Johnson Lake, where you can also enjoy one of Banff’s only beaches and boating on the lake.</p>
<p><i>Access: Johnson Lake parking lot, off the Lake Minnewanka Scenic Drive</i></p>
<p><i>Length: 2.8 km loop</i></p>
<h4><b>9. Lake Louise Lakeshore </b></h4>
<p>The beautiful <a href="https://www.banfflakelouise.com/lake-louise">Lake Louise </a>region is at a higher elevation than Banff and it takes longer for the snow to disappear. While hikers need to be careful in this region (there is some avalanche potential on the trails!), the lakeshore trail is the first to open up, and it’s a great place to wander while the snow melts up high.</p>
<p><i>Access: Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise waterfront</i></p>
<p><i>Length: 4 km return</i></p>
<h4><b>10. Johnston Canyon</b></h4>
<p>This interpretive walkway follows the edge of <a href="/johnston-canyon">Johnston Canyon</a> and leads hikers to the stunning, cascading waters of the Lower and Upper Falls. Keep your eyes out for interpretive panels that provide information along the route, as well as unique rock formations shaped over centuries by the surging water.</p>
<p><i>Access: Johnston Canyon parking lot, Bow Valley Parkway. Please check road closures prior to travel.</i></p>
<p><i>Length: 2.4 km return to the lower falls and 5 km return to the upper falls</i></p>

<h4><b>A few early season hiking tips</b></h4>
<p>1. Hard-packed snow at low elevations may be slippery, but don’t be deterred! Consider renting some ice cleats/micro-spikes from a local gear store (try <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Snowtips-Bactrax Ski &amp; Bike Rental" data-gtm_no_track="N" data-gtm_subcategory="Sporting goods &amp; Apparel" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2063" href="http://www.snowtips-bactrax.com" target="_blank">Snowtips-Bactrax </a>in Banff and <a data-gtm_category="Retail" data-gtm_event_value="1" data-gtm_label="Wilson Mountain Sports" data-gtm_subcategory="Sporting goods &amp; Apparel" data-gtm_trigger="website" data-memberid="2467" href="http://www.wmsll.com" target="_blank">Wilson Mountain Sports</a> in Lake Louise) for a good grip.</p>
<p>2. Prior to heading out, check the Parks Canada website for <a href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/tcond/cond_e.asp?oPark=100092">trail conditions</a>, <a href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/scond/Rec_Rep_e.asp?opark=100092">important bulletins</a> and <a href="http://www.pc.gc.ca/apps/scond/Rec_Rep_e.asp?opark=100092">planned closures</a> that might impact your plans.</p>
<p>3. You might be heading out for a short hike, but as the weather can change at any moment in the mountains, be prepared! Bring extra layers, water/food and bear spray – everything you need to stay happy and safe on the trail. </p>
<p>4. Plan ahead! Check the <a href="/travel-information">Travel Information</a> page and local operators for more information on COVID-19 restrictions and further information.</p>
